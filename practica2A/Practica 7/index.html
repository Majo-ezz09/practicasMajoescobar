London

London is the capital city of England. It is the most populous city in the United Kingdom, with a metropolitan area of over 13 million inhabitants.

Standing on the River Thames, London has been a major settlement for two millennia, its history going back to its founding by the Romans, who named it Londinium.

Paris

París is the capital city of France. It is the most populous city in the country, with a metropolitan area of over 12 million inhabitants.

Standing on the River Seine, Paris has been a major center of art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture for centuries. Its history dates back to the 3rd century BC, when it was founded by a Celtic tribe known as the Parisii. Today, it is one of the most visited cities in the world.

Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital city of Japan. It is one of the most populous metropolitan areas in the world, with more than 37 million people living in the Greater Tokyo Area.

Located on the eastern coast of Honshu island, Tokyo has grown from a small fishing village called Edo into a global center of technology, finance, and culture. It officially became the capital of Japan in 1868, when the Emperor moved the imperial residence from Kyoto to Tokyo.
